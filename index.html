About me

I'm a PhD student in Computer and Information Science and Engineering at Syracuse University's College of Engineering and Computer Science — with a 4.0 GPA, an MS in Engineering Management, and a Certificate of Advanced Studies in Project Management from SU's College of Professional Studies.

I grew up in Kingston, Jamaica, raised by a single mother who sold goods in the markets of May Pen, Clarendon. I became the first in my family to finish high school and pursue a college education. That journey — from the inner city to IBM, from uncertainty to a PhD — shapes everything I build.

I'm a GEM Scholar, IBM Fellow, and Women in Science and Engineering Fellow. I've interned at IBM, AIG, and QuickBase, and I founded STEAMfluence — a venture that grew out of my student org Scholars on a Mission into something permanent. STEAMfluence is a STEAM summer program for students of color and underserved communities, built on the belief that every young person deserves to see themselves as a future scientist, engineer, or entrepreneur. It was recognized by Syracuse University as an innovation and received a $5,000 Orange Innovation Fund grant.

My work sits at the intersection of technology, education, and community — building tools and spaces that open doors for people who weren't handed one.
