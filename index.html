About me

I'm a PhD student in Computer and Information Science and Engineering at Syracuse University's College of Engineering and Computer Science. I have acquired a BS in Computer Science, an MS in Engineering Management, and a Certificate of Advanced Studies in Project Management.

I'm a first-generation college student from Kingston, Jamaica. That journey, navigating a new country, a new culture, and a new academic world — shapes everything I build and every community I create.

I'm a GEM Scholar, IBM Fellow, and Women in Science and Engineering Fellow. I've interned at IBM, AIG, and QuickBase, and I founded STEAMfluence, a venture that grew out of my student org Scholars on a Mission into something permanent. STEAMfluence is a STEAM program for students of color and underserved communities, built on the belief that every young person deserves to see themselves as a future scientist, engineer, or entrepreneur. It was recognized by Syracuse University as an innovation and received a $5,000 Orange Innovation Fund grant.

My work sits at the intersection of technology, education, and community — building tools and spaces that open doors for people who weren't handed one.
